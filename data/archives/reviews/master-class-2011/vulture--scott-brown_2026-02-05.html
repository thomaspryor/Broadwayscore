Theater Review: The Diva Paradox of Master Class

  • By Scott Brown
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • ShareThis Counter
  • Email
Sierra Boggess and Tyne Daly in Master Class.

I can’t say that Master Class (playing at the Friedman through August 14), Terrence McNally’s wittily imagined version of the tutorials held by a phlegmatic, reminiscing, mot juste–dispensing Maria Callas at Julliard in the seventies, has ever really spoken to me. I’m no opera buff, and since I’m in a confessional mode, I'll tell you that I’m not much for “divas” either; at least, not as theatrical fetish objects. I mostly find them exhausting — the regret, the defiance, the one-liners, I get it. (Nor did I see the original 1996 production of Master Class, which won Zoe Caldwell a Tony, and then cycled through Patti LuPone and Dixie Carter; Faye Dunaway took the role on tour. Even typing all those names exhausts me; I feel like I’m hoisting each individual letter up to a 40-foot marquee.)

Master Class, produced by Manhattan Theatre Club, is at the Samuel J. Friedman Theatre.
