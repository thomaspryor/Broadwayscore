‘A Christmas Carol’ and ‘Sandra’ Reviews: Out of One, Many

Jefferson Mays dazzlingly portrays dozens of characters in a one-man rendition of the Charles Dickens holiday tale on Broadway; at the Vineyard Theatre, Marjan Neshat stars in David Cale’s suspenseful solo show about a woman investigating her friend’s disappearance

By Charles Isherwood
Nov. 23, 2022 5:25 pm ET

Solo shows, which proliferate on stages in New York and elsewhere, might seem to share a similar simplicity. After all, with but one actor at work, how much variety can this genre promise?And yet two shows now on stage in New York exemplify how radically different theatrical experiences with just one performer can be. On Broadway, Jefferson Mays is portraying more than three-dozen characters in a sumptuously designed adaptation of Charles Dickens’s beloved “A Christmas Carol.” Downtown, at the Vineyard Theatre, Marjan Neshat is starring in the writer and performer David Cale’s new show, “Sandra,” a sparely presented, twisty tale of a woman in search of a friend who has mysteriously disappeared. And the shows, both worthwhile and expertly executed, illustrate how disparately theater-makers can approach the challenge of captivating audiences with just a sole actor onstage.

A Christmas Carol

Nederlander Theatre, 208 W. 41st St., New York

$79.75-$173.50, ticketmaster.com, closes Jan. 1, 2023

Sandra

Vineyard Theatre, 108 E. 15th St., New York

$35-$99, 212-353-0303, closes Dec. 18

Dickens is among the greatest delineators of characters who leap from the pages of a novel into our hearts and minds. And there is possibly no American actor more suited than Mr. Mays to shuffling through the dozens of players in Dickens’s celebrated tale with the ease of a cardsharp. In the Tony-winning “A Gentleman’s Guide to Love and Murder”—also set in period England—Mr. Mays played a merry murderer as well as several of the victims, male and female, he dispatched in the pursuit of a fortune.
