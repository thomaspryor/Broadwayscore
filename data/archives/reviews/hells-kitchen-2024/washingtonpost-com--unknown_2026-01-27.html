The Alicia Keys musical, ‘Hell’s Kitchen,’ is nice, and nothing more

23 songs by the popular singer-songwriter tell the coming-of-age story of a New York teenager with a musical gift

November 19, 2023More than 2 years ago
4 min
Make us preferred on Google
Review by Peter Marks

NEW YORK — In “Hell’s Kitchen,” they’ve crafted a perfectly nice musical out of the soul-fired songbook of Alicia Keys. Ali, a character inspired by the teenage Keys and played by newcomer Maleah Joi Moon, is strong-willed but nice. Her boo, Knuck (Chris Lee), is a bit standoffish but also really nice. Despite her hard shell, Ali’s mother, Jersey (Shoshana Bean), seems pretty darn nice, too. As for Ali’s unreliable, mostly absent father, Davis (Brandon Victor Dixon): You’d have to grant that, at some level, he’s kind of — well, you get the picture.
