Review: Performances of ‘Comedy of Tenors’ and ‘Bandstand’

By Michael Sommers

  • Oct. 23, 2015

Semi-fresh may be the best term to describe two new shows that are currently running in New Jersey. The first is a comedy that is rooted in an earlier work. The second is a new musical that embraces the dark side of a bygone era.

Considerable laughter can be found in Princeton, where the McCarter Theater Center is presenting Ken Ludwig’s “A Comedy of Tenors” in a premiere coproduction with the Cleveland Playhouse.

Whether or not one is acquainted with Mr. Ludwig’s “Lend Me a Tenor,” this sequel to that popular hotel room farce packs a good deal of amusement.

The characters from the original who reappear here in a deluxe hotel suite in Paris in 1936 are Saunders (Ron Orbach), a cranky impresario from Cleveland; Max (Rob McClure), his trusty assistant who not so incidentally also sings; Tito (Bradley Dean), the temperamental Italian opera star; and Maria (Antoinette LaVecchia), Tito’s spitfire wife.

Saunders is backing a sold-out concert headlined by Tito, Max and — after the tenor Jussi Bjorling unexpectedly cancels — Carlo (Bobby Conte Thornton), a rising luminary who is secretly romancing Tito’s daughter Mimi (Kristen Martin). The moody Tito’s jealousy over Carlo’s popularity and his mistaken belief that Maria and Carlo are enjoying an affair soon send him storming out.
