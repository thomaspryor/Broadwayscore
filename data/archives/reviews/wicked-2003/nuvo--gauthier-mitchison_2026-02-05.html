Review: "Wicked" from Broadway in Indianapolis

“Wicked” is at Old National Centre/The Murat through July 6. Go to https://indianapolis.broadway.com/shows/wicked/ for details and tickets. 

No doubt bolstered by the success of the first installment of “Wicked” on the big screen, the musical is just as pop-uuu-lar as when it premiered in 2003. Opening night’s Wizomaniacs in the audience weren’t shy about causing a disturberance for their favorite Ozmopolitan gals, including encouragerizing catcalls and “You go, girl!” -- often considered disrespectation in the the-ah-tah, but here it is a manifestorium of how new audiences have discoverated -- and responderated to -- “Wicked,” no matter how swankified the venue. But, no matter how many times you’ve seen it, hearing those first notes and seeing the Clock of the Time Dragon coming to life are always thrillifying. 

Love me some neologisms. 

In “Wicked,” the linguification is a fun, subtle reminder of its storybook origins, injecting a sort of childishness into the book/musical/film's more mature interpretation of Oz’s witches and their motivations. Like Amy’s perpetual mispronunciations in “Little Women,” our reaction is an adult’s indulgent but amused smile at a child's struggle with vocabulistics, creating the vertiginous effect of feeling old and young at the same time. 

In the current tour, Zoe Jensen plays Glinda and, June 18-22, Elphaba was played by Olivia Valli (June 24-July 6, the part will be played by Jessie Davidson). No disrespectation to director Joe Mantello or Valli, but I wasn’t a fan of Elphaba’s initial portrayal. She was much too coltish -- a slightly cringy adult trying to act like a kid. However, Valli absolutely nailed “Defying Gravity,” which is notorious for being one of the most demanderating musical belts ever. From there, Valli’s Elphaba showed more poise as her character’s arc played out.  

Jensen is a perfect personification of Galindafication; and the supporting characters and large ensemble are spot-on. 

Gratitution for another chance to see the musical that turns a classic story sideways so we can see it from a greenified point of view. 

You can check out the Ozian Glossararium online.
