Goings On About Town: The Theatre

Lombardi

Print E-Mail Feeds

 

Lombardi

In Eric Simonson’s new play, based on a biography by David Maraniss, “When Pride Still Mattered: A Life of Vince Lombardi,” and directed by Thomas Kail, a young sports reporter (Keith Nobbs) spends a week in 1965 with Vince Lombardi (Dan Luria), the infamously gruff coach of the Green Bay Packers, and his long-suffering wife, Marie (Judith Light). Lombardi is a lot like the reporter’s father: intolerant of imperfection, too demanding; and the reporter is a lot like Lombardi’s son (and teammates): uncontrollable and imperfect; and the week is explosive. It’s hard to believe that in 2010 we can still romanticize selfish, abusive bastards, even if they’re gifted enough to turn a losing team into winners. That said, this has the potential to be a moving story about fathers and sons and football, but Luria would do well to tone down the yelling and play more subtle notes. The dazzling sound and lighting turn a simple stage into a pro-football field, and Judith Light cuts through a loud, cold men’s world with her warm brilliance.
