Come Back, Little Sheba

By Time Out editors Wed Jan 30 2008

WHAT A CARD Anderson plays tricks for Merkerson. Photograph: Joan Marcus

Time Out Ratings

<strong>Rating: </strong>3/5

“Being busy is being happy,” says the efficient Mrs. Coffman (Brenda Wehle) to her next-door neighbor Lola (Merkerson) in the Broadway revival of William Inge’s 1950 hit, Come Back, Little Sheba

The veins of Inge’s domestic melodrama bulge with the blood of pop psychology—sang-Freud, if you will—from the relationship between Lola and Doc (she has a childlike affect and calls him “Daddy”) to the naked erotic symbolism of Marie’s boyfriend Turk (Brian J. Smith), a javelin thrower (“It’s a big, long lance. You hold it like this, erect”). In 1950 this was heady stuff, and Inge’s depiction of violent alcoholism must have been genuinely shocking as well. But in the Manhattan Theatre Club’s technically accomplished production, directed by Michael Pressman, the machinery of the writing is always visible, and Lola—despite Merkerson’s skill—comes off as something of a simp. Come Back, Little Sheba doesn’t leap into the present tense: Yesterday’s play of the year has become a movie of the week.

—Adam Feldman

Biltmore Theatre By William Inge. Dir. Michael Pressman. With S. Epatha Merkerson, Kevin Anderson. 2hrs 10mins. One intermission.
