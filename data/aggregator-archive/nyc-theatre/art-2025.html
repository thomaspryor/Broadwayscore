What Critics Are Saying About Broadway's Art

Saadiqah, September 23rd, 2025
Share
Tweet
Email
Share
Share

The play is currently at Music Box Theater until December!

The Broadway play starring Tony winners James Corden, Neil Patrick Harris, and Bobby Cannavale had the critics talking, let's see what they had to say!

Reviews

The Guardian

"Corden is superb, a perfectly calibrated level of hysterical, sincere and crisp."

TheaterMania

"Art is as smart a commentary on friendship, identity, and the unspoken expectations that tie them together as it ever was."

"Between Corden and Harris, you'd think Ellis's production might be in danger of running out of scenery to gobble up. But rest assured, Harris's Serge remains a man of manners whose lapses must first pass through a sieve of refined grammar and fine tailoring."

New York Theatre Guide

"Art has humor, a wonderful cast, and the timeless clash of differing opinions."

The Daily Beast

"The clever thing about Art is that it doesn't indulge easy philistinism, simply deriding the painting and Serge for buying it."

"In Art, ultimately, the work of art is everything and nothing."

New York Stage Review

"The three performers mesh together beautifully"

"The three performers mesh together beautifully, with Harris providing just the right haughty snobbishness, Cannavale making comic exasperation into an art form, and Corden so lovable and vulnerable you can almost forget how nasty he can be to waiters in real life."

APNEWS

"Sure, inflation has hit Broadway tickets, too, but this "Art" is a howl. Bring a friend."

"this "Art" is a howl"


Don't miss this powerhouse trio as they star as three friends whose relationship is tested when one of them buys a controversial piece of art.
