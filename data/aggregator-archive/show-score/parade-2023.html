About the Show

A Broadway transfer of the dramatic musical about the trial of Leo Frank, based on a true story.

Leo and Lucille Frank are a newly married Jewish couple striving to earn a living in the old red hills of Georgia. When Leo is accused of an unspeakable crime, it thrusts the pair into an unthinkable test of faith, humanity, justice and devotion. Thrilling and gloriously hopeful, this show reminds us that to love, we must truly see each other.

Parade is Jason Robert Brown and Alfred Uhry's musical dramatization of the true life 1913 trial, conviction, legal appeal, and eventual lynching of Jewish factory manager Leo Frank of Marietta, Georgia.

The star-studded staging of Parade features leading performances by Emmy, Grammy and Tony Award winner Ben Platt (Dear Evan Hansen) as Leo Frank, and Micaela Diamond (The Cher Show) as his young wife Lucille, reprising their roles in this Broadway staging of the Audience Acclaimed production that premiered in concert at New York City Center in 2022, directed by Michael Arden (Once on this Island, Deaf West's Spring Awakening). Read reviews for that run here!

Recommended for audiences ages 13 and up.
