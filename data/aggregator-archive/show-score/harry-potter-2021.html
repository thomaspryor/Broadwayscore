About the Show

Harry Potter is all grown up in this Tony-winner that brings the magic of the books alive on stage.

Harry Potter, now a husband and a father, works for the Ministry of Magic. His youngest son Albus is starting school at Hogwarts and struggling with the legacy of the family name. But when Harry's past comes back to haunt him, and Albus disappears, events are set in motion that will change the world as they know it. Harry, Ginny, Hermione and Ron are back to face the dark magic and, with the next generation of wizards, they must defeat it.

The play by Jack Thorne won six Tony Awards and nine Olivier Awards, including for Best Play. There is a lot of stage magic and special effects.

When it opened on Broadway in 2018, it played in two parts. It reopened as a one part play in 2021, after the Broadway pandemic shutdown.
